Before recording

Here we are at Mushroom Studios Rayleigh, getting ready for filming. I met one of the guitarists of a music band by chance. I went to a music store in Southend On Sea. I asked one of the staff if they knew of any music bands that might be interested in doing a music video. Fortunately, the first guy I asked was James, the guitarist of the band "Under One Sun." We then share emails to do all the coordination for the production of the music video. Although there were some inconveniences days before the recordings since it was the second time that I attended the studio. The first time we had to cancel the recordings because some members of the band were late in arriving and I chose to leave the place, thinking of making a different video clip with another artist, but two weeks later they suddenly call me in the afternoon and they told they were going to play in the studio that night and this time all the members would attend. I was in London and very tired, but I decided to go because I knew that it was an opportunity that I couldn't pass up since I had already done the storyboard before.

I made this storyboard to use as a shot list. My idea was to do six takes of the song, which means the band had to play the same song six times. I then added six video layers to the timeline (Software: Adobe Premiere), to mix them, but I decided in the end not to use take 1 because it was static and boring.

Once the video editing was done, I used some free-stock videos from "pexel.com" to create a narrative according to the lyrics of the song.

Finally, I added colour effects so that it changes from reddish (original) to blue in the studio. See below
